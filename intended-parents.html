For Intended Parents

Your journey,
clearly explained.

We know the surrogacy process can feel overwhelming. This page walks you through every stage — timelines, costs, legal protection, and what to expect as an international family in California.

The six stages
Timeline

18–24 months,
start to birth.

Every journey is different, but this is the typical arc. We set honest expectations at every stage so there are no surprises.

01

Initial consultation

A confidential 30-minute call or video meeting. We listen to your situation, explain the California process, and answer every question — no obligation.

02

Application & onboarding

You complete a brief profile so we understand your family type, medical history, preferences, and timeline. Our team reviews and confirms the right next steps.

03

Surrogate matching

We present fully screened surrogate profiles — medical records, psychological evaluations, background checks included. You meet candidates at your own pace until the right match feels right.

04

Legal contracts & escrow

Independent California-licensed attorneys represent each party. A contract is signed, escrow is established, and all compensation is managed transparently through a third-party trust account.

05

IVF transfer & pregnancy

We coordinate with your fertility clinic for embryo transfer. Once pregnancy is confirmed, we provide ongoing support — OB appointments, weekly check-ins, and a dedicated case manager.

06

Birth & legal parentage

California's pre-birth order ensures your names appear on the birth certificate. You are recognized as legal parents before delivery — no adoption, no court battle.

International families

You don't need to live in the U.S. to build your family here.

Lumen has extensive experience guiding families from mainland China, Taiwan, Hong Kong, Singapore, and other countries through California surrogacy. We provide Mandarin-language support throughout.

  • California's pre-birth order is valid regardless of parents' nationality or country of residence.
  • Your child will be a U.S. citizen by birth and receive a U.S. passport.
  • We coordinate virtually — most of the process requires only 1–2 trips to California.
  • We work with immigration attorneys experienced in international birth planning.
  • All documents provided in English and Chinese.
  • WeChat communication available for day-to-day updates.

In California, gestational surrogates have no legal claim to the child. Because the baby is not genetically related to the surrogate, and because a pre-birth order is issued by the court before delivery, the intended parents are the legal parents from the moment of birth. California law is designed to protect all parties — including the surrogate.
